When rendered, CSS and JavaScript will display the video in an embedded player, allowing users to play, pause, and control the video directly on the page.

When to use

  • When showcasing a single video or a featured piece of audiovisual content.
  • To provide a quick, embedded viewing experience without navigating away from the page.
  • For tutorials, product demos, or marketing materials that rely heavily on video.

When to consider another block

  • If you need multiple videos presented in a rotating or gallery format (e.g., Carousel).
  • When you only need to provide a link or reference to external media without embedding.
  • If you require a more comprehensive layout with additional text or media elements.
